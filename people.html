Principal investigator

Jaewook (Jake) Lee, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Geophysics
Director, Geophysics & Geomodeling Lab
Geology Graduate Program Coordinator
Department of Chemistry and Geosciences, The University of Texas Permian Basin

lee_j@utpb.edu Google Scholar

Dr. Jaewook (Jake) Lee joined UTPB as a tenure-track Assistant Professor in August 2024. He received his Ph.D. in Geosciences from The University of Texas at Dallas in 2022, where he developed statistical and machine learning approaches to the seismic rock physics of organic-rich shales with Prof. David Lumley. Before joining UTPB, he was a Postdoctoral Fellow with the Texas Seismological Network (TexNet) at the Bureau of Economic Geology, The University of Texas at Austin, where he built basin-scale 3D velocity and geomechanical models of the Permian Basin for induced seismicity research. He also holds an M.S. in Earth Resources and Environmental Engineering from Hanyang University and a B.S. in Earth and Environmental Sciences from Korea University.

His research integrates geophysics, rock physics, and artificial intelligence to characterize and monitor subsurface energy systems, with applications to CCUS, induced seismicity, unconventional resources, and geothermal energy.
