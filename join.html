Open positions for M.S. students and undergraduate researchers.

Join our team

We are always looking for curious and motivated students who want to work at the intersection of geoscience, data science, and energy. Prior experience in programming or geophysics is helpful but not required; a strong work ethic and a willingness to learn matter most.

Prospective M.S. students

Our lab admits students through the M.S. in Geology program at UTPB. Graduate researchers in our group work on active research projects in carbon storage, induced seismicity, basin-scale modeling, and machine learning, often in collaboration with the Bureau of Economic Geology, industry partners, national laboratories, and other universities. Financial support is available for qualified students through research assistantships, teaching assistantships, and scholarships, depending on funding availability.

Practical research with real data

Hands-on research with industry-scale well log and seismic datasets, addressing real subsurface problems in the Permian Basin.

Industry software

Training with industry-standard software (Petrel, Techlog, Kingdom, HampsonRussell, RokDoc, PaleoScan) and GPU computing.

Networking and career pathways

Connections with industry, national laboratories, and universities. Our students and postdoc have moved on to internships, Ph.D. programs, and research positions.

Close mentoring

Close mentoring and professional development with academic, laboratory, and industry partners.

Undergraduates

Undergraduate researchers

UTPB undergraduates are welcome to join the lab for research experience through independent study, summer research programs, and funded initiatives such as the NSF Research Experience for Undergraduates and the HSI-STEM program. Undergraduate research is a great way to prepare for graduate school or an industry career.

How to apply

Please email Dr. Lee at lee_j@utpb.edu with (1) a brief statement of your research interests and career goals, (2) your CV or resume, and (3) unofficial transcripts. Information about admission to the M.S. in Geology program is available on the UTPB Graduate Admissions website.
