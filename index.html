About the lab

Welcome

Welcome to the Geophysics & Geomodeling Lab at The University of Texas Permian Basin (UTPB). Our lab, directed by Dr. Jaewook (Jake) Lee, develops data-driven and physics-based methods to characterize, image, and monitor the Earth's subsurface. We integrate seismic data, well logs, rock physics, and machine learning to build 3D and 4D earth models that support carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS), induced seismicity analysis, geothermal energy, and responsible resource development.

Located in the heart of the Permian Basin, the most prolific oil and gas province in the United States, our lab offers students a rare opportunity to work on real-world subsurface problems using industry-scale datasets, commercial software, and high-performance computing.

What we do

Three things define our group

AI-enabled geoscience

We build interpretable machine learning and physics-informed deep learning models that turn well logs and seismic data into reliable subsurface predictions.

Permian Basin focus

We lead DOE-funded carbon storage research and basin-scale 3D modeling in one of the most active energy basins in the world, right outside our door.

Industry partnerships

We train students with commercial software and data through academic partnerships with SLB, S&P Global, Ikon Science, GeoSoftware, Eliis, and TGS.
